Reverse-Seared Steak

Description

Reverse-Seared Steak is a steak cooked in the oven before on the pan

Ingredients

  • Steak, at least 1 inch in thickness
  • Neutral Cooking Oil
  • 3 cloves of minced garlic
  • Rosemary
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Tablespoon of butter

Steps

  1. Let steak dry on a wire rack in fridge overnight or up to 24 hours
  2. Preheat oven to 250 degrees farenheit
  3. Coat a stainless steel or cast iron pan in oil.
  4. Place steak on pan. Put oven in preheated oven. Use temperature probe to measure temperature at thickest point, avoiding bone.
  5. While steak is cooking, season cutting board with rosemary, salt, pepper, butter, and minced garlic
  6. Bake until steak registers at 115 internally. Double check with instant-read thermometer.
  7. Put pan on stovetop on high heat. Careful to avoid touching the hot handles with bare hands.
  8. Cook until even brown crust is formed on all sides. Make sure to sear sides.
  9. Place steak on cutting board on top of seasoning. Cut into slices, then mix up by hand to absorb the flavours.
